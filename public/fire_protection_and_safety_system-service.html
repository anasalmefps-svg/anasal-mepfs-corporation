Fire Protection & Safety Systems

Comprehensive fire detection, suppression, and protection solutions

What is Fire Protection & Safety Systems?

Fire Protection & Safety Systems is a critical engineering discipline focused on the safeguarding of human life and property from the destructive effects of fire and smoke.

It involves the strategic integration of active systems—such as fire alarm networks, automatic sprinklers, clean agent suppression, and kitchen fire suppression units—and passive features—like fire-resistant barriers, compartmentation, and escape routes. The primary goal is to detect fires early, control or extinguish them rapidly, and ensure the safe evacuation of occupants, minimizing risk and ensuring business continuity.

Core Objectives

  • Life Safety & Evacuation
  • Property & Asset Protection
  • Business Continuity
  • Environmental Protection
  • Early Fire Detection
  • Suppression & Control

Fire Alarm System

A Fire Alarm System is an integrated network of detection devices, control panels, notification appliances, and signaling equipment designed to detect the presence of fire, smoke, or heat in a building and alert occupants for immediate evacuation.

The system operates as the first line of defense in any fire emergency. Upon detecting smoke or temperature rise, it triggers audible sirens, visual strobes, and can simultaneously relay signals to a central monitoring station or fire department, ensuring rapid emergency response before the fire escalates.

Core Objectives

  • Early fire and smoke detection
  • Occupant warning and evacuation
  • Emergency response coordination
  • Zone identification and monitoring
  • Integration with suppression systems
  • Real-time status and fault reporting

Fire Protection System

A Fire Protection System encompasses a comprehensive set of active and passive measures engineered to prevent, control, and extinguish fires within a facility. It includes automatic sprinkler systems, standpipe systems, fire pumps, water storage tanks, hose reels, and fire hydrants.

Unlike detection systems that only warn, fire protection systems actively fight the fire. They are designed based on hazard classification, hydraulic calculations, and occupancy type to deliver the correct water pressure, density, and coverage needed to suppress fire at its origin before it spreads to adjacent areas.

Core Objectives

  • Automatic fire suppression
  • Structural integrity preservation
  • Containment of fire spread
  • Support for firefighting operations
  • Compliance with fire codes
  • Minimization of property loss

FM200 Suppression System

An FM200 Suppression System is a clean agent fire suppression system that uses heptafluoropropane (HFC-227ea) gas to rapidly extinguish fires in enclosed spaces without leaving residue, making it ideal for protecting sensitive equipment and critical infrastructure.

FM200 works by absorbing heat energy from the fire, disrupting the combustion chain reaction. It discharges as a gas within 10 seconds, reaching the required design concentration to suppress Class A, B, and C fires. Because it leaves no residue, it is the preferred choice for server rooms, data centers, telecommunication facilities, and electrical switch rooms where water-based systems would cause catastrophic damage.

Core Objectives

  • Rapid fire extinguishment
  • Zero residue on equipment
  • Safe for occupied spaces
  • Environmentally acceptable agent
  • Protection of critical assets
  • Minimal downtime after discharge

Kitchen Fire Suppression System

A Kitchen Fire Suppression System is a specialized wet chemical fire suppression system designed specifically for commercial and industrial kitchens to automatically detect and extinguish grease and oil-based fires originating from cooking appliances, exhaust hoods, plenums, and ductwork.

Unlike general-purpose systems, kitchen suppression uses specially formulated wet chemical agents that react with burning grease to form a soap-like foam layer, sealing the surface and preventing re-ignition. The system simultaneously shuts off gas and electrical fuel supplies to the cooking equipment, addressing the fire at its source and stopping it from reigniting after suppression.

Core Objectives

  • Grease and oil fire suppression
  • Prevention of fire re-ignition
  • Automatic fuel source shut-off
  • Hood and ductwork protection
  • Safe for kitchen equipment
  • Compliance with kitchen fire codes
